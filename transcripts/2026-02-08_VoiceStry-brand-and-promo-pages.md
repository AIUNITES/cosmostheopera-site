## Domain Naming Strategy Discussion

### Names Evaluated and Rejected

**Too clinical/anatomical (throat stigma):**
- ThyroLaryn, CricoThyroid Notations, MusculoVocal, Laryngeal Harmony
- These lean INTO the discomfort instead of abstracting away from it

**Too generic/crowded:**
- Acoustic Insights, Vocal Dynamics, The Sound Stage, Vocal Signature

**Too metaphorical/vague:**
- The Singer's Palette, Sonic Canvas, The Vocal Bridge

**Decent but not great:**
- Voice Blueprint, The Singer's Map, Vocal Compass
- VoiceChart (short, clear, but might be taken)
- Vocal Architect (strong but long)
- NotePoint (clean but confusable with presentation apps)

### Key Insight: "Resonance" and "Notation" Too Clinical
- Independent musicians hear "notation" and think sheet music they can't read
- "Resonance" signals physics class
- "Method" sounds like something you can learn
- "-Stry" sounds like something you can do

### Domains Acquired

**vrnmethod.com**
- Professional/educational entry point
- Voice teachers, choral directors, SLPs
- "Method" slots into existing mental category (Suzuki Method, Mel Bay Method, Faber Method)
- Professionals comfortable with acronyms (IPA, SOVT, SLP)

**voicestry.com**
- Consumer/creator entry point
- YouTube viewers, indie singers, podcasters, church choir singers
- No jargon â€” sounds like a place to work on your voice
- Double reading: "VoiceStry" (craft/foundry) + "VoicesTry" (invitation to try)
- Matches AIUNITES naming pattern (BizStry)
- Where "5 Gears" and "Bodybuilding for Your Voice" content lives

### Two-Door Strategy
- VoiceStry for discovery (consumers, creators, YouTube audience)
- VRN Method for depth (professionals, educators, researchers)
- Both lead to same system, cross-linked
- YouTube vocal coach crowd finds VoiceStry
- Voice teacher looking for framework finds VRN Method
